Frog Stand

This is where the journey begins. By building a solid foundation in the frog stand or crow pose. While more of a balance skill than a strength skill, it acclimates you to putting weight through your wrists and helps you overcome the fear of falling forward.

Straight Arm Frog Stand

This skill introduces additional balance components, as well as furthering the conditioning of the wrists. Forward lean increases compared to the bent-arm version. This is also the first stage in which the arms are kept completely straight.

Tuck Planche

With the legs completely removed from the body, the shoulders now bear the entirety of the load. Work on keeping the legs tucked as close as possible to the chest, the scapula protracted, and the lean consistent.

Advanced Tuck Planche

The back is now flattened out. Due to this decreased leverage, the load on the shoulders is even higher.

Straddle Planche

The strength requirements keep increasing at this stage, straddling the legs outward to provide additional stress on the upper body.

Half Lay/One-Leg-Out Planche

While both of these variations are viable, half-lay is preferred due to the requisite strictness and it's resemblance to the full planche.

Full Planche

The final destination in this series of skills is extremely difficult. It requires incredible shoulder strength and full body tension to pull off.
